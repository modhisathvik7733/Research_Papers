## What I learned (the big one)
My "two goals" — cut training cost, and continual learning without forgetting —
are not two goals. **Continual learning IS the cost-reduction mechanism**: the
$100k retrain only exists because we throw the model away and retrain from
scratch. That's the whole thesis. I hadn't connected them.

Also: as a solo researcher with an M1 Max, I cannot compete at 7B from-scratch.
The open contribution surface is (a) treating *training cost* as a first-class
metric that CL papers usually ignore, and (b) understanding the *mechanism* of
forgetting at tiny scale where I get 30+ runs/week.

Also learned (corrected misconception): I thought training cost is *mostly the
optimizer*. It isn't — the optimizer is a constant-factor knob (~2×). Cost is
dominated by model size × data, data quality, and how often you retrain from
scratch. Full intuitive write-up: [docs/notes/training-cost.md](../docs/notes/training-cost.md).
The instinct ("cost has one dominant cause") was right; the cause was wrong —
and the right causes (don't retrain from scratch; need fewer examples) ARE the
thesis.

## What surprised me / didn't match my prediction

problem. The A100 is a *month-3* concern, not a now concern.
