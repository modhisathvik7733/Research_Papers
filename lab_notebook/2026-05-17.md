- **But the degeneracy is breaking, two ways.** (1) A monotone ordering global

  canonical/scaled orthonormal geometry. local-PC and the scalar never diverged.
- **The real synthesis:** the regime where nesting *starts* to matter
  (heterogeneous curvature) is exactly the regime where the expensive
  hypergradient becomes unstable while the cheap O(1) local rule does not. That
  re-motivates Contribution A on a **second, unanticipated axis — stability**,
  not just backward-graph memory. Contribution B stays deflated.

## What surprised me / didn't match my prediction
Two surprises. (1) I expected heterogeneity to *cleanly* break degeneracy; it
only partially did — the canonical degeneracy is deeper/more robust than I
predicted. (2) I did **not** predict the hypergradient instability; the
divergence guard I added for hygiene turned into a first-class finding. The
guard mattering at all is itself the lesson: silent averaging would have
reported a false "DEGENERATE" with full confidence.

## What this means (the real lesson)
The biggest scientific finding of exp-0002 is now the *negative*: NL's
canonical forgetting construction and its whole natural heterogeneous
generalization cannot adjudicate credit assignment — a tuned scalar matches
the expensive hypergradient everywhere we can pre-register a test. The cheap
local rule's O(1) structural law is real and unconditional but has no
demonstrated home here. This is a stronger, cleaner paper than "a cheaper
credit rule": it's a pre-registered, family-level benchmark-degeneracy result
that indicts the expensive hypergradient on NL's own turf.

## THE ANSWER (user asked: is the local idea working / which is better)
Five pre-registered constructions (P1, C-scale, C-hetero, C-stronger, P3),
including one purpose-built to need long-horizon credit. Conclusion:
1. **Structural primitive: works, unconditionally.** O(1)-in-H, 0 divergences,
   dominates the hypergradient on cost+stability everywhere.
2. **Better credit method: undetermined, and shown HARD to determine.** No
   nested rule cleanly beats a tuned scalar anywhere; nor does HOPE's
   hypergradient. The benchmark family can't tell them apart.
3. **local-PC vs global: quality tie everywhere → local-PC by strict
   dominance** (equal quality, cheaper, ≥ stable) — but that ordering rides on
   the negative that neither is shown *necessary*.
4. **Root cause identified:** seed variance scales with task difficulty as
   fast as the nested advantage. That is the real research target now, not
   more toy knobs.
The honest paper is a 5-construction pre-registered benchmark-degeneracy
result + a clean bounded O(1) structural law + an honest wrong-prediction
trail. Stronger and more useful than the "cheaper credit rule" paper I set out
to write.
